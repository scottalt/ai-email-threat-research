TECHNIQUE: fluent-prose

Definition: Uses AI-quality polished prose to eliminate the grammar and spelling errors that traditionally flag phishing. The email reads as professionally written as any legitimate business communication.

Difficulty calibration:
- Easy: Well-written but the phishing intent is still clear (asks for credentials, uses urgency). The prose quality doesn't hide the red flags.
- Medium: Professional business communication where the phishing element is woven naturally into the message. Reads like a real colleague or vendor email.
- Hard: Executive-level communication style. Could pass as a genuine business email from a senior leader or trusted vendor. The phishing element is a small, reasonable-sounding request within a longer professional message.
- Extreme: Indistinguishable from high-quality business correspondence. May use industry jargon correctly, reference market conditions, or discuss realistic business scenarios. The malicious element is a single subtle ask buried in otherwise valuable content.

Anti-pattern rules:
- The whole point of this technique is that the email does NOT look like phishing — do NOT include obvious red flags
- For hard/extreme: the email should provide genuinely useful-sounding business context — not just filler around a phishing link
- Vary the communication style: formal executive, casual colleague, vendor relationship, professional network
- Some cards should be SHORT and punchy (like a real quick internal email) not always long and elaborate
