TECHNIQUE: pretexting

Definition: Constructs a detailed false scenario or cover story to justify the request. The scenario provides context that makes the malicious ask seem logical and expected.

Difficulty calibration:
- Easy: Pretext is thin — a single claim without supporting detail ("We detected unauthorized access to your account"). The ask doesn't match the scenario well.
- Medium: Pretext has multiple supporting details that create a coherent story. The scenario feels plausible but the ask is slightly out of proportion.
- Hard: Elaborate, multi-paragraph pretext with specific details — dates, reference numbers, prior interactions. The malicious request feels like the natural next step in the story.
- Extreme: The pretext is indistinguishable from a real business scenario. Could be a vendor dispute, an insurance claim follow-up, a compliance audit, or a contract renewal. The ask is completely reasonable within the story. Only domain analysis reveals the fraud.

Anti-pattern rules:
- Do NOT make pretexts sound like they were written by someone trying to sound official — they should sound like a real person writing a real email
- Hard/extreme pretexts should include mundane details that add credibility (referencing previous emails, specific amounts, dates)
- The ask should feel like a natural consequence of the story, not bolted on at the end
- Vary scenarios: insurance claims, vendor onboarding, IT migration, employee onboarding, legal review, audit compliance
