CATEGORY: marketing (legitimate)

Definition: Promotional emails — sale announcements, product launches, newsletter content, event invitations, loyalty program updates. These are real marketing emails from companies the user has a relationship with.

These are NOT phishing.

Difficulty calibration:
- Easy: Standard promotional email with clear branding, specific offers, unsubscribe link. Obviously a newsletter.
- Medium: Marketing email with a compelling call-to-action ("Claim your reward", "Limited time offer — act now") that uses urgency-like language. But it's genuine marketing from a real company.
- Hard: Marketing email that looks like it could be phishing — "Your account has a pending reward", "Click to activate your exclusive offer", "Your membership benefits are about to expire". Uses urgency and action language, but it IS legitimate marketing.
- Extreme: Marketing email from a third-party service on behalf of a company (uses unfamiliar sending domain), with aggressive CTAs, personalized with user's name and purchase history. Looks very suspicious but is genuine.

Realism rules:
- Marketing emails often come from third-party ESPs with unfamiliar domains — this is normal: via.sailthru.com, email.shopify.com, t.send-notifications.com
- Include realistic marketing elements: specific discount percentages, product names, event dates
- Some should reference user's "account" or "membership" — legitimate companies do this
- authStatus can be "unverified" (common for bulk marketing through third-party ESPs)
- Some should have slightly pushy language — real marketing IS pushy, that doesn't make it phishing
