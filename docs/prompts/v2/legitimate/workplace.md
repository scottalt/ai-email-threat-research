CATEGORY: workplace (legitimate)

Definition: Internal business emails — meeting invites, project updates, policy changes, IT announcements, HR communications, team updates. These are real internal emails from colleagues and departments.

These are NOT phishing.

Difficulty calibration:
- Easy: Standard internal email with clear context — meeting notes, project update, team announcement. Obviously from a real colleague.
- Medium: Internal email that references systems or asks for action — "Please update your details in the HR portal", "Complete your compliance training by Friday". Sounds like it could be phishing but is genuine.
- Hard: IT or security department email about a real system change — "We're migrating to a new SSO provider, click here to set up your new account", "Your VPN certificate expires next week — renew here". Uses the exact language phishing would use, but it's genuinely from IT.
- Extreme: Executive email that feels like CEO fraud — "I need you to review this document before the board meeting tomorrow" or "Can you process this invoice for me? I'm in back-to-back meetings." But it's genuinely from the executive. Requires trusting the sender domain and context.

Realism rules:
- Use internal-sounding domains: firstname.lastname@acmecorp.com, it-helpdesk@meridianhealth.org, hr@techflow.io
- Internal emails are often SHORT and informal — not every email needs to be long
- Some should reference real-sounding internal systems: "Workday", "ServiceNow", "Confluence", "Jira"
- IT/security emails that ask users to take action (change password, update MFA, install update) are LEGITIMATE and players need to learn to trust them when the domain is correct
- authStatus should be "verified" for internal emails
