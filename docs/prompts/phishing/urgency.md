TECHNIQUE: urgency

Definition: Creates false time pressure or threatens immediate negative consequences to prevent careful analysis. The goal is to make the recipient act before thinking.

Difficulty calibration:
- Easy: Urgency language is explicit and prominent ("Act within 24 hours or your account will be permanently deleted", "IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED"). Sender domain is suspicious. The urgency is disproportionate to the stated cause.
- Medium: Urgency is present but professional in tone ("We've placed a temporary hold on your account pending verification"). Context is plausible. Sender domain looks legitimate at first glance.
- Hard: Urgency is subtle and embedded in routine-sounding correspondence. Time pressure feels natural ("Our systems will undergo scheduled maintenance on Friday — please update your payment method before then"). No obvious red flags on first pass — requires careful analysis to detect. The phishing mechanism (suspicious sender domain or account action ask) must still be present and detectable on close reading; highlights should surface the subtle mismatch between the stated routine purpose and the actual credential or account access request.
- Extreme: The urgency is invisible on first read. The deadline is embedded as a passing detail in otherwise unremarkable correspondence — a single sentence noting a cutoff date buried in a longer email. The sender domain is near-perfect (one transposed character, legitimate-looking subdomain, or alternate TLD). A casual or even careful reader would see only a routine operational notice. Only systematic, character-level analysis of the sender domain and precise critical reading of the action requested would surface the phishing nature.

Generation notes:
- Easy: Include urgency keywords in caps or with exclamation marks, use a clearly suspicious domain
- Medium: Professional tone, make the urgency feel like a service-protection measure
- Hard: Urgency should feel routine. The harder the card, the less it should read like phishing on first pass.
- Extreme: The entire email reads as legitimate operational communication. The urgency is a quiet deadline, not a scare. The only tells are forensic-level: sender domain discrepancy and a credential or account action buried in routine language.
- Vary the context: banking, subscription services, email accounts, cloud storage, HR systems
